# SEO Competitor Round - 2026-06-29

## Summary

This round studied established Singapore tuition competitors and applied the highest-impact pattern we were missing: a broad commercial landing page for Singapore tuition and private tutor matching.

The site already had subject pages and blog articles, but it did not have a strong head-term page aimed at searches such as `Singapore tutor`, `Singapore tuition`, `private tutor Singapore`, and `tuition agency Singapore`. Competitors rank with exactly that type of page.

## Competitors Studied

1. [SmileTutor](https://smiletutor.sg/)
2. [FamilyTutor](https://familytutor.sg/)
3. [Tutor City](https://tutorcity.sg/)
4. [MindFlex](https://mindflex.com.sg/) was checked, but its SSL/certificate response was inconsistent from the terminal environment.

## What They Are Doing Well

### 1. Exact commercial intent targeting

Their homepage titles and headings use phrases close to what parents search:

- `Home Tuition Agency`
- `Home Tutors in Singapore`
- `Private Tutors in Singapore`
- `#1 Trusted Home Tuition Agency in Singapore`

Why this helps: Google can quickly understand that the page is a direct answer for high-intent commercial searches, not just a generic education brand page.

### 2. Strong parent decision flow

The top competitors do not only describe tuition. They show:

- How to request a tutor
- How matching works
- What subjects and levels are covered
- Whether the service is fast
- Reviews and testimonials
- Tuition rates or market-rate guidance
- FAQs

Why this helps: These sections satisfy both SEO intent and conversion intent. A parent can decide faster, and Google sees comprehensive topical coverage.

### 3. Large internal link footprint

Competitor pages link into many subject, level, exam, and service pages.

Why this helps: Internal linking distributes authority from the homepage or commercial hub into deeper pages, helping Google discover and understand the full tuition topic cluster.

### 4. Trust and proof are visible

Competitors put trust signals near the commercial journey:

- Reviews
- Testimonials
- Featured tutors
- Improvement claims
- Media mentions
- Long operating history

Why this helps: Tuition is a trust-heavy purchase. Parents are not buying a worksheet; they are trusting someone with their child's performance. Trust signals reduce hesitation and can improve engagement.

### 5. FAQ coverage

Competitors answer common parent objections directly, often near the bottom of the page.

Why this helps: FAQ sections capture long-tail queries, improve topical completeness, and can support structured data.

## Gaps Found On EliteMind Tutor

1. No broad `/singapore-tuition` page existed for head-term searches.
2. The homepage was focused on brand positioning and IB/IGCSE, but not enough on broad `Singapore tuition` and `private tutor Singapore` search intent.
3. Subject pages existed, but they were not grouped into a strong commercial hub.
4. The sitemap did not include a top-priority Singapore tuition landing page.
5. The navigation did not expose a direct tuition hub link.

## Changes Applied

### New commercial landing page

Added `/singapore-tuition` with:

- SEO title targeting `Singapore Tuition Agency`, `IB`, and `IGCSE`
- H1 targeting `Singapore Tuition & Private Tutors`
- Parent-focused matching process
- Trust and fit explanation
- Subject and curriculum internal-link hub
- Tuition rate expectation section
- FAQ section
- FAQ structured data
- Educational service structured data
- Request tutor and WhatsApp CTAs

### Homepage internal links

Added a `Popular tuition searches` block to the homepage linking to:

- `/singapore-tuition`
- `/ib-tuition`
- `/igcse-tuition`
- `/math-tuition`
- `/physics-tuition`
- `/online-tuition`

### Navigation

Added a `Tuition` link in the desktop navigation and `Singapore Tuition` in the mobile menu.

### Sitemap

Added `/singapore-tuition` with weekly change frequency and high priority.

## Why This Should Help SEO

1. The site now has a page built for broad commercial search intent instead of only specialist and blog intent.
2. Google gets clearer topical signals around `Singapore tuition`, `private tutor Singapore`, `IB tuition`, and `IGCSE tuition`.
3. Internal links from the homepage and new hub strengthen the subject pages.
4. FAQ schema improves machine-readable page understanding.
5. The page is conversion-oriented, so any traffic that lands there has a clear route to submit a tutor request.

## Scorecard Impact

Estimated score movement: `34 / 100` to `38 / 100`.

This is a structural improvement, not an immediate ranking win. Google still needs to crawl, index, and test the page. Real movement should be measured through Google Search Console impressions, clicks, and indexed page count.

## Next Best Moves

1. Add a tuition rates page or expand the rates section into a dedicated `/tuition-rates-singapore` page.
2. Add FAQ schema to `/ib-tuition`, `/igcse-tuition`, and major subject pages.
3. Add stronger tutor profile examples and proof points.
4. Publish location or curriculum cluster pages only where they are genuinely useful, such as `international school tutor Singapore`.
5. Connect Google Search Console data so competitor-inspired changes can be judged by impressions and clicks, not guesswork.
